About Afolashade

Afolashade Joy Jubrilla is a governance and development specialist, author, and future-of-work voice focused on how people, institutions, and economies stay relevant in an era of rapid technological change.


With experience spanning banking, central operations, commercial lending, portfolio management, risk, compliance, international trade, and mobility services, she brings a rare systems-level perspective to conversations about digital transformation, workforce readiness, and human judgment in automated environments. Her work sits at the intersection of policy, operations, and people.


She is the author of Surviving the AI Recession, a book that explores why performance alone is no longer enough, and why adaptability, analytical thinking, and decision clarity are becoming the true currencies of relevance in the digital age. Drawing from real-world organisational shifts, layoffs, restructuring, and evolving hiring systems, her writing speaks to professionals, leaders, and institutions navigating uncertainty.


Afolashade holds a Master’s degree in Governance and Development Policy from ISS Erasmus University, The Hague. She is a strong advocate for human-centered innovation, inclusive growth, and lifelong learning. Her work consistently emphasizes that technology should amplify human capability, not erase it.


Beyond her professional work, she is committed to social impact. She advocates for continuous education for young girls, stands firmly against child marriage, and supports body positivity and dignity in an increasingly digital and judgment-driven world. She believes people should be free to live, grow, and work without being reduced to labels, biases, or automated decisions.


Her voice is calm, analytical, and grounded in lived experience. Her mission is clear: to help individuals and institutions build relevance, resilience, and clarity in a world that refuses to stand still.
